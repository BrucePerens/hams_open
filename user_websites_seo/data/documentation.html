User Websites SEO: Optimization Guide

Optimize your blog visibility without technical complexity.

What is SEO? Search Engine Optimization (SEO) helps search engines like Google understand your blog content so it can be shown to the right people.

🚀 Quick Start: Optimizing Your Blog

You can manage how your blog appears in search results directly from the "Site" menu on your blog page.

  1. Navigate: Go to your personal blog or your group's blog page.
  2. Open Menu: Locate the Site menu at the top of the screen.
  3. Select Tools: Click on Optimize SEO.
  4. Update Info: Fill in the Title and Description. These are what people see on Google!
  5. Save: Click "Save" to apply your changes immediately.

🛠️ Advanced Controls

For more granular control, you can also access SEO settings via your user profile or the group configuration page in the backend. These settings allow you to fine-tune how search engines and social media platforms interact with your blog.

  • SEO Name: This is a special, search-engine friendly name for your blog. It helps search engines understand the topic of your site just by looking at the link.
  • Keywords: You can add specific words or phrases that describe your blog. This helps search engines categorize your content so it shows up when people search for those terms.
  • Open Graph Image: This is the preview image that shows up when you or someone else shares a link to your blog on social media sites like Facebook, X (formerly Twitter), or LinkedIn. A good image can make people more likely to click on your link!

🛡️ Safety & Security

This module is built with privacy and security in mind. It uses a "Zero-Sudo" architecture, which means:

  • Privacy: You only have permission to edit SEO data for your own blog.
  • Collaboration: Group members can only edit SEO data for their shared group blogs.
  • Protection: We use a microscopic privilege system. This means your SEO settings are isolated. Even if a bad actor tried to use these settings to gain more access, our security system would block them automatically.
