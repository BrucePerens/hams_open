Manual Library User Guide

Version: Odoo 19 Community
Summary: A powerful, easy-to-use knowledge base for your Odoo system.

1. What is Manual Library?

The Manual Library is a comprehensive documentation system for Odoo. It allows you to create, organize, and publish articles, guides, and SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) in a hierarchical structure. It is designed to be fully compatible with the knowledge.article data model while remaining entirely open-source.

2. Navigating the Manual

You can access the manual through the Manuals menu in Odoo or via the public website at /manual.

  • Sidebar Navigation: On the left side of the screen, you will find a tree-view of all accessible articles. You can expand and collapse folders to find the documentation you need.
  • Search: Use the search bar at the top of the manual page to find articles by title or content.
  • Table of Contents (TOC): For longer articles, a dynamic Table of Contents is generated on the right side. Clicking on a heading in the TOC will jump you directly to that section.

3. Creating and Editing Articles

To create or modify documentation, you must have the appropriate permissions:

  1. Navigate to Manuals > Articles in the Odoo backend.
  2. Click New to create a new article or select an existing one to edit.
  3. Title: Give your article a clear, descriptive title.
  4. Icon: You can add an emoji or icon class to help visually distinguish the article in the sidebar.
  5. Parent Article: Use this field to nest the article within a hierarchy. Leaving it blank makes it a root article.
  6. Body: Use the integrated rich-text editor to compose your content. You can use headings (H1, H2, etc.), which will be used to generate the Table of Contents.
  7. Sequence: Adjust the sequence number to change the order in which articles appear in the sidebar.

4. Permissions and Privacy

Manual Library offers flexible access control via the Internal Permission field:

  • Read Only: All internal users can view the article.
  • Read & Write: All internal users can view and edit the article.
  • No Access (Private): The article is only visible to the creator and specifically invited members.

To invite specific users to a private article, use the Shared Members list.

5. Publishing to the Web

By default, articles are only visible to logged-in Odoo users. To make an article available to the general public (unauthenticated users):

  1. Open the article in the backend.
  2. Click the Is Published toggle button in the top-right corner.

Note: For a child article to be visible on the website, its parent articles should also be published or accessible.

6. Feedback and Quality Control

At the bottom of every article on the website, users can indicate whether they found the information helpful. This feedback is recorded anonymously and can be viewed by administrators in the backend to identify articles that may need improvement.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use images and videos in my articles?
A: Yes! The Odoo rich-text editor supports embedding images, videos, and links directly into the article body.

Q: What happens if I delete a parent article?
A: To protect your data, the system uses "Restrict" on deletion. You must first move or delete the child articles before you can delete the parent.

Q: Is there a limit to how deep the hierarchy can go?
A: There is no hard limit, but for the best user experience, we recommend keeping your structure organized and not overly deep.
