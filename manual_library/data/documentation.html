Manual Library User Guide

Version: Odoo 19 Community
Summary: A powerful, easy-to-use knowledge base for your Odoo system.

1. Introduction

The Manual Library provides a robust, hierarchical documentation system within Odoo. Whether you're creating internal standard operating procedures (SOPs), software manuals, or public-facing help guides, this module offers the tools to organize and publish your content effectively. It is built to be a drop-in open-source replacement for the Odoo Enterprise Knowledge app, ensuring data compatibility and a familiar workflow.

2. Navigating the Manual

There are two primary ways to interact with the Manual Library:

  • Internal Backend: Accessed via the Manuals app icon in the Odoo main menu. This is where administrators and contributors create and manage articles.
  • Public Web Portal: Accessed at the /manual URL on your website. This provides a clean, reader-friendly interface for browsing published documentation. Portal users and internal staff can also access unpublished internal documents if granted permission.

Sidebar and Tree View

On the left-hand side of the manual portal, a navigation sidebar displays the article hierarchy. Articles are categorized into three sections for logged-in users:

  • Workspace: Articles shared with everyone in the organization, including authenticated Portal users with read access.
  • Shared: Private articles that have been specifically shared with you or your department.
  • Private: Your personal drafts and notes that are not visible to others.

Search and Discovery

A powerful search bar at the top of the /manual page allows you to find articles instantly by searching through titles and content. Search results respect your permissions—you will only see articles you have the right to read.

On-Page Navigation (TOC)

For extensive articles, a dynamic Table of Contents (TOC) is automatically generated on the right side of the page. It picks up your H2 and H3 headings, allowing readers to jump directly to relevant sections.

3. Authoring Documentation

Creating high-quality documentation is straightforward using the Odoo backend:

  1. Navigate to Manuals > Articles.
  2. Click New to start a fresh article.
  3. Title and Icon: Enter a title. Use the Icon field to add an emoji (e.g., 🚀, 💡, 🛠️) to make the article stand out in the sidebar.
  4. Content: Use the rich-text editor to write your guide. Use standard formatting like Bold, Italic, and especially Headings to structure your content.
  5. Hierarchy: Select a Parent Article to nest your new article under an existing one. This creates the tree structure seen in the sidebar.
  6. Sequence: Articles are ordered by their sequence number (lowest first). Adjust this to manually sort articles within the same level of the hierarchy.

4. Visibility and Access Control

Manual Library uses a granular permission system to ensure the right people see the right content. This is controlled by the Internal Permission field on each article:

  • Read Only: Every internal user (staff) and authenticated Portal user can see the article but cannot edit it.
  • Read & Write: Every internal user can see and edit the article. This is great for collaborative wikis.
  • No Access: The article is hidden from everyone except the creator and people listed in the Shared Members tab. Both internal and portal users can be granted access here.

5. Publishing to the Web

To make your documentation available to customers or the general public without requiring them to log in:

  1. Open the article in the backend.
  2. Click the Is Published button in the stat-box area (top right).

Important Note: For a child article to be visible to the public, it must be published. While child articles can be accessed directly via their URL if published, they will only appear in the public sidebar if their parent articles are also accessible (either published or workspace-level).

6. Feedback Loop

At the bottom of published articles, users can vote with a "Helpful" or "Not Helpful" button. This anonymous feedback helps documentation managers identify which guides are clear and which might need a rewrite. You can track these counts directly on the article form in the backend.

7. Tips for Great Manuals

  • Use Clear Headings: This helps both the reader and the automatic Table of Contents.
  • Keep it Visual: Embed screenshots and diagrams directly into the body.
  • Link Between Articles: You can highlight text and link to other manual articles to create a truly interconnected knowledge base.
  • Monitor Feedback: Regularly check your articles' helpfulness ratings to prioritize updates.
