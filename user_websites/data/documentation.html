User Websites Module Documentation

Version: Odoo 19 Community
Summary: This module allows community members to create and manage their own personal or group websites and blogs directly within this Odoo instance. It provides a simple way to share content while maintaining privacy and security.

1. System Configuration (For Administrators)

Administrators can manage how the module behaves globally by going to Settings > General Settings > User Websites.

  • Global Page Limit: This sets the maximum number of web pages a regular user can create by default. You can also set a specific limit for an individual user on their profile page.
  • User Websites Administrators: Choose which users have permission to manage group sites, review abuse reports, and lift suspensions.

2. Using Your Personal Website

Every member of our community can have their own corner on the web. Here is how you can get started and manage your content.

2.1. Setting Up Your Site

  • Your Personal URL: Your website is located at /<your-username>/home.
  • Initialization: To create your site, simply visit your personal URL while you are logged in. If you haven't started yet, you will see a "Welcome" page with a button to "Create Your Home Page". Click it to generate your initial layout.
  • Designing Your Pages: Use Odoo's built-in "Edit" mode to drag and drop blocks, change text, and add images. Don't forget to click "Save" when you are finished.

2.2. Starting Your Blog

Your personal blog is located at /<your-username>/blog. Just like your main site, it starts as a placeholder. Visit your blog URL and click "Create Your Blog" to publish your very first post.

2.3. Showing Up in the Community Directory

We value your privacy. By default, your website is hidden from the public list. If you want others to find your site more easily:

  1. Click on your user icon in the top right and select My Profile.
  2. Go to the User Websites tab.
  3. Check the box for "Show in Public Directory" and save your profile.

2.4. Group Shared Websites

Clubs, teams, or project groups can also share a website.

  • These sites have their own unique URL, similar to personal sites.
  • Any member of the group has full editing rights and can post to the group blog.

2.5. Following Other Sites

If you enjoy another community member's content, you can click "Subscribe" on their site. This will sign you up for a weekly email digest that summarizes any new blog posts they have published. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in the email.

2.6. Reporting Inappropriate Content

To keep our community safe, every page has a Report Violation button (look for the red flag icon). If you see something that breaks our rules, please report it. Our administrators will review the report and take action if necessary.

Privacy Note: The owner of the website cannot see who reported them. Reports are completely confidential and seen only by administrators.

2.7. Appealing a Suspension

If your account is suspended for rules violations, your content will be hidden. You can submit an appeal through your main account dashboard (/my/home) to explain the situation to administrators.

2.8. Your Data Rights: Exporting Content

You can download a full copy of all your website and blog content in a computer-readable format by visiting the Privacy & Data section in your account dashboard (/my/privacy).

2.9. Your Data Rights: Deleting Your Content

If you decide to leave our platform, you can permanently delete all your websites, blog posts, and personal data through the Privacy Dashboard. Please be careful: this action cannot be reversed.

3. Developer and Technical Information

3.1. Security Architecture

  • Just-In-Time Provisioning: Website and blog records are only created when the user first visits their slug, ensuring no wasted database space for inactive users.
  • Zero-Sudo Design: The module uses restricted Service Accounts to perform background tasks like creating pages and unpublishing content, ensuring that no user ever has more power than they strictly need.
  • Automated Security Shield (XSS & SSTI Neutralization): To keep the platform safe, every page you build is automatically scanned by our security engine. If the system detects malicious code (like <script> tags or dangerous Odoo QWeb directives), it will automatically strip them out. Note: Attempting to bypass these security checks is a violation of our community rules and will result in an automatic moderation strike against your account.

3.2. Extension Points (Dropzones)

Developers can add new features to the user websites by targeting these specific structural IDs in their own modules:

  • Home Header: user_websites_dropzone_home_header
  • Home Footer: user_websites_dropzone_home_footer
  • Navbar Actions: user_websites_dropzone_navbar_actions
  • Directory Card: user_websites_dropzone_directory_card
  • Blog Post: user_websites_dropzone_blog_post
  • Settings: Inherit res.config.settings view.

3.3. Scheduled Background Tasks

  • Weekly Digest: Automatically sends summaries of new posts to followers.
  • Moderation Alerts: Notifies administrators when there are new abuse reports to review.
  • View Counter Sync: High-performance view tracking that saves data from memory to the database periodically.

This module uses the knowledge_docs feature to ensure that this manual is always available to you directly within the system.

5. Compliance Summary

  • Privacy: We use privacy-friendly counters instead of cookies for tracking views.
  • Accessibility: Our templates follow standard accessibility rules to ensure they are usable by everyone.
  • Moderation: We provide transparent reporting and appeal processes to maintain a healthy community environment.
