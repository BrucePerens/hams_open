Site Owner's Guide to Regulatory Compliance

A comprehensive guide for community administrators regarding GDPR, CCPA, ePrivacy, WCAG, and platform moderation regulations.

1. Introduction

Running a website today involves many legal responsibilities. The compliance module is designed to automate the technical side of these responsibilities so you can focus on your community. This guide explains what the module does and how you can manage your site's legal standing.

2. Data Privacy & Protection (GDPR & CCPA)

Modern data protection laws like the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in Europe and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) mandate strict handling of personal data. This platform is built with Privacy by Design.

  • Data Minimization: The platform avoids unnecessary data collection. System view counters operate without logging Personally Identifiable Information (PII) or IP addresses.
  • Data Portability (Right to Access): Users have the right to request a copy of their data. The platform provides an automated JSON export tool at /my/privacy.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Users must be able to delete their accounts and data. An automated hard-delete facility is available at /my/privacy, which immediately unlinks all authored pages and blogs.

3. Cookie Consent (ePrivacy Directive)

The ePrivacy Directive (often called the "Cookie Law") requires explicit user consent before storing non-essential cookies (like analytics or marketing trackers) on a user's device.

  • Native Integration: This module automatically enforces Odoo's native Cookie Consent Bar across all active websites upon installation. It also ensures that any new websites you create will have this feature enabled by default. [@ANCHOR: compliance_post_init_cookie_bar]
  • How to Verify: To see the cookie bar in action, open your website in a "Private" or "Incognito" browser window. You should see a banner at the bottom of the page asking for your permission to use cookies.
  • Compliance Protocol: Do not bypass this bar by hardcoding third-party tracking scripts into your website templates. All trackers must hook into the core framework's consent state.

4. Required Legal Boilerplates

To operate legally, your website must publicly display specific policy documents. The compliance module automatically provisions editable, AGPL-3 compatible templates for:

  • Privacy Policy (accessible at /privacy): Explains data collection, usage, and user rights. [@ANCHOR: compliance_privacy_policy_template]
  • Cookie Policy (accessible at /cookie-policy): Details the types of cookies used and how users can manage them. [@ANCHOR: compliance_cookie_policy_template]
  • Terms of Service (accessible at /terms): Outlines the rules for using the platform, content ownership, and moderation policies. [@ANCHOR: compliance_terms_of_service_template]

How to Verify and Customize These Pages

You can find and edit these pages using the standard Odoo Website tools:

  1. Go to your website.
  2. Navigate to the URL (e.g., yourdomain.com/privacy).
  3. Click the Edit button in the top right corner.
  4. Use the Odoo website builder to update the text to reflect your specific organization's details.

The "Non-Destructive Mandate"

We respect your work. If you have already created a custom page at one of these URLs before installing this module, we will not overwrite it. Instead, the module will automatically "unpublish" its own boilerplate version to ensure your custom content remains the one your visitors see. [@ANCHOR: test_compliance_non_destructive_mandate]

If you want to go back to using our boilerplate after having unpublished it (or having it automatically unpublished), simply delete your custom page or unpublish it, and then re-publish the "Global Compliance" version of the page in the Site -> Pages menu. The module's background processes will also automatically re-publish its boilerplate if no custom version is detected for that specific website.

Additionally, because these pages are created as "noupdate" records, any edits you make using the website builder will be preserved even when you update the compliance module in the future.

5. Web Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) ensure that web content is accessible to people with disabilities. Many jurisdictions now legally require WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

Our platform is designed to facilitate compliance, but the responsibility for accessible content remains with the site owner. Key features include:

  • Semantic HTML: The legal page templates use proper tag hierarchy (e.g., <h1>, <h2>, <p>) to ensure screen readers can navigate the content logically.
  • Keyboard Navigability: All standard interactive elements are navigable via the Tab key with visible focus states.
  • ARIA Labels: Where necessary, elements are equipped with aria-label attributes to describe their function to assistive technologies.

6. Platform Moderation (Digital Services Act / DSA)

Regulations governing user-generated content, such as the EU's Digital Services Act (DSA), require platforms to have transparent, accessible, and fair moderation processes. While the compliance module doesn't handle moderation itself, our platform architecture supports these requirements through other integrated modules:

  • Notice and Action Mechanism: Integrated modules provide a "Report Violation" button on user-generated content, allowing guests and users to report abuse.
  • Reporter Shielding: To prevent retaliation, the identity of the complainant is strictly shielded from the content owner.
  • Fair Appeals: The automated Three-Strike suspension system implemented across the platform ensures users can contest moderation decisions fairly.

Module Compliance Statement: Global Compliance

How the compliance module complies with the above regulations:

  • ePrivacy: Directly enforces the activation of the core cookies_bar upon installation.
  • Transparency: Automatically provisions the required legal boilerplate pages utilizing standard Odoo website pages.
  • WCAG: The generated QWeb templates for the legal pages utilize semantic HTML structures and respect standard theme contrasts.
